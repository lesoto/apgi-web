1

Core Profile View (Always Visible)

Radar Chart Visualization

Primary visualization showing mental model parameters at a glance.

Why this stays:
One-glance mental model, shows balance vs imbalance, intuitive shape recognition. This is the identity view of the dashboard.

Axes Configuration

The radar chart displays these four key parameters:

  • ε - Sensitivity to environmental signals
  • π - Processing intensity
  • θₜ - Awareness threshold
  • β - Body vs world focus balance

Data Overlay

Comparative visualization showing user data in context.

  • User score - Your current parameter values
  • Population average - Displayed as a faded overlay for comparison
2

Numeric Reality Check

Bar Chart Design

Four bars, normalized 0–100 for each parameter.

Labels:
Low / Medium / High categorization with hover text explaining each parameter in plain language.

Purpose

Grounds the abstract radar chart in concrete numbers.

Key Functions:
Reduces misinterpretation of the radar chart, appeals to users who distrust abstract shapes, provides concrete reference points.
3

Awareness & Overload Indicator

Ignition Gauge Display

Single composite gauge showing current mental load vs awareness threshold.

Behind the scenes:
Calculated as ε × π compared to θₜ (sensitivity × processing intensity vs awareness threshold).

Visual representation of different mental states.

Purpose:

4

Body vs World Focus

Balance Visualization

Slider-style visualization showing the continuum between body-focused and world-focused attention.

Scale Endpoints:
Left: Body-focused (internal awareness)
Right: World-focused (external awareness)

Animated Transitions

Smooth animated transitions between different states of somatic balance.

Purpose:
Makes the β parameter concrete, explains phenomena like panic, flow, and dissociation without clinical labels.

Parameter Toggles

Users can toggle individual parameters on/off to focus on specific aspects of their mental model.

Purpose:
Shows that the user is not broken, normalizes natural fluctuation, introduces the concept of mental plasticity.
6

Summary Panel

Auto-generated, short, direct interpretations:

"You notice small problems quickly."

"Your system trusts threat signals more than reward."

"Awareness fills easily under stress."

"Experience is strongly body-centered right now."

Important Design Principle

No advice. No therapy language. Only descriptive statements based on the visualization data.
