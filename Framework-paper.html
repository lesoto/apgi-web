Allostatic Precision-Gating
of Conscious Access

A Computational Framework Linking Predictive Processing, Interoception, and Global Workspace Dynamics

In an energy-constrained brain, how does the nervous system decide which information gains global access — rising into awareness — and which remains unconscious? APGI proposes a formal answer: conscious access occurs when accumulated precision-weighted prediction errors, with interoceptive signals amplified by somatic markers, exceed a dynamically regulated threshold. This framework integrates the Free Energy Principle, interoceptive predictive processing, and Global Neuronal Workspace Theory into a single, falsifiable computational model.

Formal Definition

The Precision-Gating Equation

St = Πᵉ·|zᵉ| + Πⁱ_eff·|zⁱ| > θ_t
where  Πⁱ_eff = Πⁱ_baseline · exp(β·M(c,a))
S_t is the accumulated precision-weighted surprise at time t. When it exceeds the dynamically adjusted threshold θ_t, global neuronal ignition occurs — information becomes conscious.
Framework Parameters

Four Variables. One Threshold.

Π

Interoceptive Precision

The inverse variance of prediction error distributions. Bodily signals receive preferential weighting when homeostatic stability is threatened — precision amplifies internal signals above external noise.

|ε|

Prediction Error

The mismatch between predicted and actual sensory signals — both exteroceptive (world) and interoceptive (body). Large errors carry more weight. Surprise drives the system toward ignition.

Bₜ

Ignition Threshold

The dynamic gate that must be crossed for conscious access. Actively modulated by metabolic state — the brain adjusts its own sensitivity based on energy availability and allostatic demands.

M

Somatic Marker

Learned interoceptive associations that modulate neural gain. Somatic markers encode embodied memory of past homeostatic states, shaping which signals receive amplified precision-weighting.

Theoretical Contribution

Three Gaps. One Unified Framework.

Prior accounts of consciousness left three critical explanatory gaps unresolved. APGI bridges all three with a single computational architecture.

Computational Gap

How is threshold set?

Previous models lacked a mechanistic account of what determines the ignition threshold. APGI formalizes threshold as dynamically regulated by metabolic state and allostatic demands.

Embodied Gap

Why does the body matter?

Consciousness research neglected interoception. APGI integrates somatic markers and interoceptive precision-weighting as primary modulators of what reaches global access.

Metabolic Gap

Why is it so expensive?

Global neuronal broadcast costs 5–10% extra glucose in frontoparietal-thalamic networks. APGI explains this via the energy-information trade-off at threshold crossing.

  • 4

  • 5
