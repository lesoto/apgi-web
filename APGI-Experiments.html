Core Architecture

Four fundamental computational models driving consciousness research

θₜ

Dynamic Threshold

Adaptive ignition threshold mechanism for conscious access. Gates information flow based on interoceptive precision and prediction error magnitude.

π

Precision Weighting

Precision-weighted prediction errors that modulate sensory processing. Determines confidence in interoceptive vs exteroceptive signals.

ε

Prediction Errors

Hierarchical prediction error signals across multiple timescales. Drives learning and conscious awareness through surprise minimization.

F

Free Energy

Expected free energy calculations for decision making. Balances epistemic value (information gain) with pragmatic value (goal achievement).

Validated Experiments

Rigorous experimental paradigms with empirical grounding

Interoceptive Gating Paradigm

Tests how interoceptive precision gates conscious access through cardiac discrimination tasks.

  • Three conditions: interoceptive focus, exteroceptive focus, control
  • Threshold tracking and detection rate analysis
  • Simulates heartbeat detection accuracy
  • Validates precision-gating hypothesis
python run_experiments.py interoceptive_gating \
--n_participants 20 --n_trials 100

AI Agent Benchmarking

Compares different agent architectures in survival environments with multiple performance metrics.

  • Tests Random, Reactive, DQN, and APGI agents
  • Grid world with food, obstacles, and predators
  • Metrics: survival time, energy efficiency, food consumption
  • Demonstrates APGI advantages in complex environments
python run_experiments.py ai_benchmarking \
--n_episodes 100 --n_agents_per_type 3
