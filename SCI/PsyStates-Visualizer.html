Neural Dynamics & Mechanisms

Flow represents an optimal ignition regime where the cortical reservoir achieves maximal task-relevant coherence with minimal metabolic overhead. The precision-threshold architecture enters a transient attractor state characterized by: (1) tight temporal coupling between prediction and sensory input (lag <100ms), (2) sustained dopaminergic tone maintaining stable but accessible threshold, (3) selective noradrenergic enhancement of task-relevant precision weights, and (4) reduced global competition for ignition—creating uninterrupted access for goal-aligned content. This configuration maximizes the information-theoretic efficiency ratio: relevant information accessed per unit metabolic cost. Unlike mere engagement, flow exhibits temporal entrainment (rhythmic precision-threshold oscillations phase-locked to task structure) and metacognitive suppression (reduced self-monitoring precision, enabling automatic execution).
