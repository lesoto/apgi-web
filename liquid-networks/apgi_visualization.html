APGI Framework Architecture

1 Spiking Liquid Reservoir

High-dimensional recurrent spiking network with heterogeneous neurons, sparse connectivity (~1-10%), and NMDA/AMPA/GABA receptor kinetics. Implements fading memory through synaptic decay.

2 Precision-Weighted Prediction Error

Input encoding: u(t) = Π(t) × ε(t) where precision modulates synaptic efficacy. Feedforward vs top-down mismatch drives burst firing.

3 Reservoir Dynamics

Accumulation via fading memory: dx/dt = -αx + f(W_res x + W_in u(t)). Signal S(t) = ||x(t)||² serves as ignition metric.

4 Adaptive Ignition Threshold

Dynamic θₜ regulated by arousal, metabolic state, and interoceptive bias. Implemented via inhibitory stabilization and divisive normalization.

5 Ignition Dynamics

Phase transition: dx/dt ← dx/dt + A·x·[S-θₜ]⁺. Recurrent amplification creates persistent attractor via NMDA currents.

7 Metabolic Cost Modeling

Thermodynamic costs of cognitive operations. Free energy F = Cost - Benefit determines ignition viability.

8 Neuromodulation System

NE (arousal), ACh (attention), DA (reward), 5-HT (mood) dynamically modulate network parameters.

Validation Phenomena

Bistable Perception

Identical inputs → all-or-none ignition. Demonstrates threshold-based decision making.

Attentional Blink

Second target fails to ignite within ~500ms of first due to refractory period.

Masking

Mask disrupts accumulation before θₜ is crossed, preventing ignition.

State-Dependent Access

Higher Π or lower θₜ (via norepinephrine) increases detection rate.
