Understanding APGI Parameters
The Four Core Dimensions of Conscious Processing

The Four Core Parameters:

1. Prediction Error (ε)

Magnitude of mismatch between predicted and actual sensory input. High ε = surprising, unpredictable experience. Measured across domains (body, social, sensory, motor).

2. Precision Weighting (π)

Confidence/trust assigned to prediction errors. High π = errors strongly influence belief updating and conscious access. Inverse of variance (uncertainty).

3. Ignition Threshold (θₜ)

Amount of precision-weighted prediction error (π × ε) needed to trigger global ignition and conscious access. Low θₜ = easy conscious access.

4. Somatic Bias (β)

Relative precision weighting on interoceptive vs. exteroceptive channels. High β = body-centered consciousness. Determines "where" experience is anchored.

Computational Framework:

Weighted Prediction Error: WPE = π × ε
Ignition Probability: P(ignition) = sigmoid(WPE - θₜ)
Experience Anchoring: E = β × (interoceptive) + (1-β) × (exteroceptive)
